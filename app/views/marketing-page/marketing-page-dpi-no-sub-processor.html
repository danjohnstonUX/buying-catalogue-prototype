Data processing information

Catalogue solution name: CheckUp Health Video and Online Consultation

Subject matter of the processing

The subject-matter of processing of personal data by the Processor is the provision of the Service to the Controller that involves the processing of personal data. Personal data will be subject to those processing activities which are for the purposes of providing you with healthcare and medical services and/or information relating to support relevant to you and to comply with a legal obligation and to fulfil a contractual obligation that we have with you.

Duration the processing

The Processing will take place over the duration of the relevant Service. The storage of any data item that contains detail of either system or practice operational information, that may require retention for purposes of historical review or audit, shall be retained for a period which is no longer than is necessary for those purposes, as instructed by the Service Recipients in their capacity as Controllers.

Nature and purposes of processing

For a Patient/Service User Patient/Service Users can receive support relating to their healthcare concerns when a Video and Online Consultation is the most appropriate method for delivering that support For Health or Care Professional Health or Care Professionals can conduct consultations remotely over video and online ensuring care services can be delivered in a personal way regardless of location For Healthcare Organisation Increase Patient/Service User access to advice or consultation by removing the barrier of locality. All Processing is conducted in the United Kingdom.

Types of personal data

Patients/Patients' representatives/guardians: contact details First Name Last Name Gender Password Email address

Categories of data subjects

Application and Platform and website Users. Patients, Patients' representatives/guardians, Practice/Service Users, persons working within the wider NHS and social care system. Staff (including volunteers, agents, and temporary workers), customers/ clients, suppliers, patients, students / pupils, members of the public and public bodies as required by Law.

Plan for return and destruction of the data once the processing is complete

Electronic patient records can't be destroyed or deleted for the foreseeable future. Paper: paper records are destroyed to an international standard and the organisation has an agreement in place with a third party organisation for collecting shredded waste (Shredit) They are shredded (using a cross cut shredder) under confidential conditions.

Sub processors

This solution does not use any sub processors.
