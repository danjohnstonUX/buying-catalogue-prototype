You’re not ready to start an order

You need to know the approximate value of your contract so you can follow the correct procurement procedure.

For orders worth under £40k (excluding VAT)

You can browse the Buying Catalogue and select any solution you want to order.

Your contract value should include everything you intend to spend.

It would not, for example, be acceptable to execute a number of Direct Awards to the same supplier to avoid carrying out a competition.

More information about executing a Direct Award procedure (opens in a new tab).

For orders between £40k and £250k (excluding VAT)

You must carry out a competition of similar solutions using information available on the Buying Catalogue. Score solutions based on how they perform against certain criteria, for example, price, implementation timescales or service levels.

When executing a competition, you cannot create requirements that result in a shortlist of one Catalogue Solution.

More information about executing an On-Catalogue Competition procedure (opens in a new tab).

For orders over £250k

You must invite shortlisted suppliers to provide written tenders. You can select the solution you want to order based on this information.

More information about executing an Off-Catalogue Competition procedure (opens in a new tab).

Once you have carried out one of these procurement processes and identified the most appropriate solution, you’ll be ready to place your order.
